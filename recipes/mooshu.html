Mooshu Recipe

Description

Mooshu is a cabbage and carrot vegetable stirfry, which can have pork or beef added to it. The stirfry is wrapped up in thin pancakes and dipped in a molasses, peanut butter, and soy sauce mixture called hoisin sauce.

Mooshu is not difficult to make and is a nice change from stirfry with rice.

Ingredients

Stirfry:

  • Cabbage
  • Onions
  • Carrots
  • Eggs
  • Pork

Pancakes:

  • 1 3/4 Cups Flour
  • 3/4 cup boiling Water

Instructions

Steps for Stirfry:

  1. Cut vegetables and meat into thin strips.
  2. Stirfry in a few tablespoons of oil in a hot pan, preferably a wok.

Steps for Pancakes:

  1. Mix flour and water with wooden spoon, then knead until smooth on a floured board, about 3 minutes.
  2. Cover with a towel and allow to rest for 1/2 hour.
  3. Shape dough into a cylinder about 12 inches long.
  4. Cut roll into 1-inch pieces and shape them back into rounds.
  5. Flatten pieces into 2-inch rounds with palms.
  6. Oil each round and pair them off.
  7. Roll each sandwich on a floured board into one thin pancake, 7 inches across.
  8. Cook each pancake in an ungreased iron pan over medium-high heat, about 1 minute on first side, 30 seconds on second side.
  9. While pancake is still hot, separate it into two very thin pancakes. Cover with a towel to keep from drying out.
